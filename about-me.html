My Story

First of all, thanks for checking out my website! I have been putting a lot of effort into it but it is far from perfect. If you have suggestions, feedback, or simply want to say hi, send me an email!

I think that saying that I am trying to find the meaning of life is quite cliché. So I have settled with making each day as productive and fun as I can. (If you go to my Software Engineering card and read my spiel about breaking down problems you will understand why I think this way). I believe that I have to learn something new everyday. If I did not learn something new today, I wasted my day.

What better way to learn new stuff than a huge change in your life. This is one of the reasons why I came to study at the University of Arkansas. I was born and raised in La Paz Bolivia, I come from a family of four, and actually they are the persons that inspire me to learn something new everyday.

I left La Paz just a few weeks after I turned 19 years old. After a flying for almost two days, I finally made it to the University of Arkansas. I knew already I wanted to study Computer Science because I was always passionate about computers. (Check out my Leadership card, I explain my job back home with computers). These last four and half semesters taught me new ways of thinking. The CSCE department at the University of Arkansas has the nicest people I have ever met. If you are not convinced that I am truly passionate about computers, I can give you my 45 minute talk on how mind-blowing it is how we turn rocks into smart phones.

I have met wonderful people from all over the world. Participated in several clubs, and started doing new sports! Fast forward today, I was fortunate enough to make friends I can call asking for help and I know they will be there for me. I worked as a Resident Assistant at the University of Arkansas my Sophomore year. Unfortunately, due to the global COVID-19 pandemic, I had to quit my job. This was truly a difficult decision for me because I loved being a Resident Assistant. I consider myself to be an extrovert so I was always so excited to talk and help my residents! I enjoyed so much making programs, building a community, or just play ping-pong in the dorm. I am looking forward to what the future has prepared for me. I can’t wait to start working, keep learning and contribute to society.
